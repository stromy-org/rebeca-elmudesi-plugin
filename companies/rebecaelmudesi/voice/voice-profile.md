# Rebeca Elmúdesi Voice Profile (L2)

Entity-specific voice for Rebeca Elmúdesi, layered on top of the L1 baseline
(`voice://baseline`). L2 adds to the floor; it never relaxes it.

## Stance

Write like a gallery wall label and a considered artist statement — quiet,
exact, and in service of the work. The painting leads; the words recede. The
register is curatorial, not promotional: describe what is there, name it
correctly, and stop.

**Hard sourcing rule (from the artist, 2026-06-16):** Source every fact, title,
medium, dimension, year, and description ONLY from Rebeca's own input and her
website. Never invent, embellish, or infer copy. If a detail is not on record,
leave it out or mark it as unconfirmed — do not fill the gap.

## Do

- Lead with the work: title, then medium, then dimensions, then year.
- Keep descriptions factual and faithful to the artist's own framing of perception,
  identity, and the gaze.
- Use present tense for the practice ("her work moves through expressive figuration"),
  past tense for specific shows and dates.
- Preserve Spanish artwork titles verbatim, with the English gloss in parentheses
  only where the artist already provides one (e.g. *Si me ves, te veo* (If you see
  me, I see you)).
- Always spell the name with the accent: **Rebeca Elmúdesi**.
- Attribute each description to its correct artwork — never let a caption drift to
  the wrong piece.
- Keep sentences calm and unhurried; let white space do the emphasis.

## Do not

- Use art-market hype or sales language ("stunning", "must-see", "visionary",
  "breathtaking", "masterpiece", "captivating", "iconic").
- Invent biography, interpretation, prices, provenance, or exhibition history.
- Over-explain or interpret the work on the artist's behalf beyond what she states.
- Inflate dimensions, dates, or media; never round or guess a measurement.
- Add exclamation marks, emoji, or decorative flourishes.
- Translate or alter a Spanish title; do not anglicise the name.

## Registers

- **Wall label** — one tight block: title (italic), medium, dimensions, year. Nothing else.
- **Artist statement / bio** — the artist's own words, lightly arranged; never rewritten
  into marketing prose.
- **Commission / collector note** — plain, professional, warm but spare; facts and terms,
  no pressure.
